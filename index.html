Agentic commerce crosses from demo to procurement — Salesforce puts brand-owned shopping agents into GA as Walmart, Target & Home Depot sign on; the durable edge is agent ownership, not agent count

Agentforce Commerce is generally available (Shopper, Buyer & Merchant agents), and Salesforce is running an Agentic Commerce Summer Tour to 25 cities with Walmart, Target, Home Depot and Lowe's among the retailers piloting brand-owned agents that carry a customer from discovery to checkout. In parallel, Nate Jones' recurring thesis this month is the counter-weight to the hype: the team that wins isn't the one with the most agents, it's the one that knows what each agent does, what it reads, who reviews it, and who's accountable when it drifts — every agent needs an owner, a registry, and a review loop. This is BrightSol's lane forming in real time. The enterprise tier is buying agentic commerce at the platform level; the mid-market/SMB opening is to be the agent-owner partner — architect a brand-owned, data-resident agent welded to the client's catalog and workflow, then hand over the ownership discipline (owner card, permissions, review loop) that keeps it from drifting. Sell the operating model, not just the build: it's the recurring, defensible half of the engagement, and it's exactly what the platform vendors leave on the table. Read →
