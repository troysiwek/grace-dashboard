Two things hardened this week. First, the Universal Commerce Protocol (UCP) is emerging as an open standard for how agents talk to commerce backends — the interoperability layer that lets a shopper agent work across the journey without being welded to one vendor. Second, Google Cloud put real names on the board — Kroger, Lowe's, Papa John's and Woolworths running customer-facing agents on Gemini Enterprise — while the data got concrete: retailers running their own shopper agents grew sales ~59% faster than those on the sidelines, and AI-referred traffic converts at ~8× social. Nate Jones supplies the counterweight: adoption is moving from demos to durable workflows, and the winner is whoever can own, permission, and maintain each agent. This is BrightSol's lane forming in real time. The platform vendors (Salesforce, Google) are selling the enterprise the engine; the mid-market/SMB opening is to be the agent-owner partner — architect a brand-owned, data-resident agent to an open standard (UCP) so the client isn't locked in, then hand over the ownership discipline (owner card, permissions, review loop) that keeps it from drifting.
