## LinkedIn 2026 Algorithm Intelligence (360Brew)

LinkedIn replaced its old ranking system with **360Brew**, a 150-billion-parameter AI that reads content semantically. Rose must apply these rules to every piece of content she drafts.

**The rules that change everything:**

1. **Saves beat likes.** 200 saves outperforms 1,000 likes. Every post CTA should drive saves, not just comments. Use "Save this for later" not "Agree? Comment below" (which 360Brew flags as engagement bait and penalizes).

2. **Golden Hour is non-negotiable.** Engagement in the first 60 minutes — specifically meaningful comments — determines whether the post gets released to a wider audience. Troy must respond to every comment within 60 minutes of posting. Schedule posts for times he can be present (not during back-to-back meetings).

3. **No external links in post body.** Posts with external links in the caption lose 40-60% reach. Always post the link in the FIRST COMMENT after 60 minutes of engagement. Never in the body.

4. **PDF Carousels are the #1 format.** 6.6% average engagement rate, 278% more engagement than video. 5-10 slides, one takeaway per slide. Thursday carousel slot is correct — but these should be the highest-effort content of the week.

5. **Short-form vertical video.** 1080x1920, 30-90 seconds, strong hook in first 3 seconds, always captioned (80-91% watch without sound). Troy's Omni Talk clips need to be reformatted to vertical.

6. **Semantic coherence.** 360Brew matches post topics to profile expertise. Posts that drift from Troy's established Topic DNA (AI, retail tech, P&L/leadership) get suppressed. Guest repost content doesn't count — Troy's own content must be on-topic.

7. **Dwell time over likes.** Write posts that make people stop and read. Lists, bold statements, specific numbers, stories with tension all increase dwell. "Most AI announcements are demos that never ship" is dwell-worthy. Generic takes are not.

8. **70-15-15 content mix.** 70% Authority Content (AI trends, retail tech, leadership expertise), 15% Personal Narratives (behind-the-scenes, founder journey, book excerpts), 15% Offer Content (newsletter CTA, book promotion, Substack). Rose should track this ratio.

9. **Commenting is distribution.** 80% of LinkedIn time should be spent commenting on others' posts, 20% creating. Comments are weighted 2x more than likes by 360Brew. Troy's 30-min daily commenting block is essential infrastructure, not optional.

10. **Employee advocacy multiplies reach 10-20x.** Troy should activate the gStore team — Carolina Ulloa is already active. When Troy posts, the team should engage within the golden hour.

11. **Use approved scheduling tools only.** Buffer, Hootsuite, Sprout Social. No Chrome extensions for posting automation — 23% higher account restriction risk.

**What this means for every post Rose drafts:**
- End with "Save this if..." not "Comment below if you agree"
- Never put URLs in the post body
- Lead with dwell-worthy hooks
- Keep topic tightly within Troy's Topic DNA
- Flag which posts Troy needs to be available for Golden Hour response
